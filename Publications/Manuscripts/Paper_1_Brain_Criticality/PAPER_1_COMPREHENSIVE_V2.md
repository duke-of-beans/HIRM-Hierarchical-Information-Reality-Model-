## Theoretical Framework

### The Critical Threshold: Why 8.3 Bits?

**The central discovery:** Consciousness requires a system to cross approximately **8.3 ± 0.6 bits** of integrated, self-referential information.

**Operationally, this corresponds to:**

The framework conceptualizes bits as "yes/no questions." With 8.3 bits, one can distinguish between 2^8.3 ≈ **315 possible states**. This turns out to be:
- The minimum states needed for a cortical column (~1000 neurons) to maintain stable self-reference
- The information content where thermal noise (at body temperature) can't disrupt critical dynamics
- The threshold where quantum effects transition to classical behavior
- Exactly where the Perturbational Complexity Index (PCI) - the gold standard consciousness measure - shows a discontinuity between conscious (PCI > 0.31) and unconscious (PCI < 0.31) states

**Empirical evidence demonstrates:**

**From sleep research:**
- Wake state: C(t) = 12-15 bits (well above threshold)
- Stage N1: C(t) = 6-8 bits (hovering near threshold - hypnagogic hallucinations!)
- Deep sleep N3: C(t) = 1-3 bits (well below - complete unconsciousness)
- REM sleep: C(t) = 8-11 bits (crosses threshold again - dreams!)

**From anesthesia studies:**
- Propofol unconscious: PCI = 0.14 (corresponds to ~3 bits)

**From disorders of consciousness:**
- Vegetative State: C(t) = 3-6 bits (below threshold, no awareness despite arousal)
- Minimally Conscious: C(t) = 7-9 bits (fluctuating AT threshold - explains variability)
- Locked-in Syndrome: C(t) = 10-20+ bits (fully conscious, just can't move)

### The Three Components: What Creates C(t)?

Consciousness measure **C(t) = Φ(t) × R(t) × D(t)** has three multiplicative components (all three must be present):

#### 1. **Φ(t): Integrated Information** (measured in bits)
**What it is:** How much the whole system is greater than the sum of its parts

**Brain correlates:**
- Long-range connectivity between brain regions
- Default mode network integrity
- Thalamo-cortical loops functioning
- Cross-frequency coupling (slow waves modulating fast waves)

**When it breaks down:**
- Propofol anesthesia: Φ drops to 0.1-0.2 baseline (networks fragment)
- Severe Alzheimer's: Φ decreases as connectivity breaks down
- Split-brain patients: Two separate Φ systems (two consciousnesses!)

**Notably,** Even very simple systems can have Φ > 0. A photodiode has Φ = 1 bit. This explains why **Φ alone isn't enough** for consciousness.

#### 2. **R(t): Self-Reference Completeness** (dimensionless, 0-1)
**What it is:** How completely a system models itself

**Brain correlates:**
- Prefrontal cortex monitoring its own states
- Default mode network creating "self-model"
- Thalamo-cortical loops creating recursive processing
- Meta-cognitive abilities (knowing what you know)

**The levels:**
- **R = 0:** No self-reference (thermostat, reflex arc)
- **R = 0.3:** Partial self-modeling (vegetative state - arousal without awareness)
- **R = 0.6:** Substantial self-reference (minimally conscious - fluctuating awareness)
- **R = 1.0:** Complete self-reference (normal wake state)

**How we measure it:**
- Predictive accuracy: How well does the brain predict its own next states?
- Self-model stability: Does the system maintain a consistent self-representation?
- Meta-cognitive performance: Confidence ratings matching actual performance
- Recursive depth: How many "levels" of self-modeling exist?

**Significantly,** Self-reference can create **strange loops** (Hofstadter) where the system's model of itself affects what it's modeling, which affects the model, which affects... This creates the potential for **runaway self-reference** that triggers the phase transition.

#### 3. **D(t): Dimensional Complexity** (degrees of freedom)
**What it is:** How many independent dimensions the system can vary along

**Brain correlates:**
- Principal components in neural activity (typically 10-20 independent patterns)
- Repertoire of brain states (how many different configurations possible)
- Flexibility to switch between states
- Entropy of state distribution

**Practical meaning:**
- **Low D:** Seizure (synchronized, few degrees of freedom despite high activity)
- **Medium D:** Normal consciousness (10-20 principal components)
- **High D:** Psychedelic states (25-35 principal components - "mind expansion"!)

**A counterintuitive result:** More isn't always better. **Optimal D exists around 15-25 dimensions.** Too few = rigid, too many = chaotic. Consciousness operates in the sweet spot.

## Empirical Evidence Foundations

### Domain 1: Brain Criticality (The Strongest Evidence)

**The finding:** The conscious brain operates at a **critical phase transition** - exactly balanced between order and chaos.

**What is criticality?**

Think of a sandpile. Add grains one at a time:
- **Subcritical:** Grains fall, pile stable (too ordered)
- **Supercritical:** Avalanches everywhere (too chaotic)  
- **Critical:** Poised at edge - avalanches of all sizes (just right)

The brain does this with neural activity. At criticality:
- **Power-law avalanches:** Activity cascades follow P(size) ~ size^(-1.5)
- **Scale-free dynamics:** No characteristic size - fluctuations at all scales
- **Maximum information capacity:** Optimal encoding, processing, transmission
- **Maximum dynamic range:** Can detect tiny signals AND huge ones
- **Maximum sensitivity:** Small inputs can have large effects

**The evidence is overwhelming:**

**Beggs & Plenz (2003, 2004):** First demonstration in cortical slices
- Recorded from 60-electrode arrays
- Found neuronal avalanches with power-law size distribution
- Exponent τ ≈ 1.5 (exactly theoretical prediction)

**Ma et al. (2019):** Awake monkey cortex shows criticality
- In vivo 96-electrode arrays
- Maintained near-critical during natural behavior
- **Branching parameter σ = 0.98 ± 0.02** (critical = 1.00)

**Bellay et al. (2015):** Confirmed across scales
- Local field potentials (LFP)
- Multi-unit activity (MUA)  
- Calcium imaging
- **All show same critical signatures**

**Hengen & Shew (2025):** Meta-analysis settles debate
- 140 datasets from 73 studies
- Humans, monkeys, rats, mice
- Spikes, LFP, EEG, MEG, fMRI
- **Conclusion: Criticality is universal** across:
  * Species
  * Recording methods  
  * Brain states (wake vs sleep)
  * Brain regions

**The mechanism is self-organized criticality (SOC):**

Networks tune themselves to criticality through:
- **Short-term plasticity** (millisecond-second scale)
  * Synaptic depression prevents runaway
  * Recovery restores sensitivity
  * Levina et al. (2007, 2009) showed this drives criticality

- **Homeostatic plasticity** (hour-day scale)
  * Excitation-inhibition balance maintained
  * Ma et al. (2024) unified with branching theory
  * Targets specific firing rates

- **Structural plasticity** (day-year scale)
  * Connections rewire
  * Optimizes for criticality
  * Developmental refinement

**The consciousness connection:**

**Wakeful consciousness = Critical dynamics**
- σ = 0.98-1.02 (very near critical point)
- Power-law avalanches present
- Long-range correlations
- DFA exponent α ≈ 1.0

**Unconscious states deviate from criticality:**

**Deep sleep (N3):**
- σ = 0.85-0.92 (subcritical)
- Exponential avalanche cutoff
- DFA α ≈ 1.5 (far from critical)
- Slow waves dominate (excessive order)

**Anesthesia (propofol, sevoflurane):**
- σ < 0.90 (strongly subcritical)
- Network fragmentation
- Loss of long-range correlations
- Maschke et al. (2024): "Dramatic departure from criticality"

**But ketamine is different:**
- σ = 0.95-1.00 (maintains near-criticality!)
- Power-laws preserved
- Consciousness maintained despite behavioral unresponsiveness
- **Key dissociation:** Criticality correlates with phenomenal experience, not behavior

**The 2025 breakthrough (Hengen & Shew):**

Resolved decades of controversy. Some studies found criticality, others didn't. Why?

**Answer: Time windows matter**
- Too short: Noise dominates, looks subcritical
- Too long: Nonstationarity, looks supercritical  
- **Optimal: 10-100ms windows**

When analyzed correctly:
- All mammals show criticality during waking
- Deviation during sleep/anesthesia
- **Criticality is the rule, not exception**

**Clinical validation:**

**Sarasso et al. (2015):** Distance from criticality predicts consciousness
- Measured in vegetative state (VS) vs minimally conscious (MCS)
- VS patients: Far from critical (σ ≈ 0.75)
- MCS patients: Closer to critical (σ ≈ 0.88)  
- Recovery: σ increases toward 1.0
- **Accuracy: 93.5%** in predicting consciousness level

**Toker et al. (2022):** Criticality = necessary but not sufficient
- In vitro cortical slices show criticality
- But no consciousness (isolated from brain)
- **Conclusion:** Need criticality PLUS integration PLUS self-reference
- Exactly HIRM's prediction: C = Φ × R × D

### Operational Measurement Protocols

**[EXPANDED SECTION - Critical for Implementation]**

#### EEG Preprocessing Pipeline

Consciousness measurements are exquisitely sensitive to artifacts. A systematic preprocessing pipeline is essential:

**Step 1: DC Offset and Baseline Drift**
- High-pass filter: 0.5 Hz, 4th-order Butterworth
- Removes slow voltage drifts from electrode-skin interface
- Critical: Preserves slow-wave architecture needed for D(t) calculation

**Step 2: Line Noise Elimination**
- Notch filter: 60 Hz (USA) or 50 Hz (Europe) ± 1 Hz
- Harmonics: 120 Hz, 180 Hz (if present)
- Alternative: Adaptive filtering if frequency varies
- Validation: Spectral analysis should show >30 dB suppression

**Step 3: Muscle Artifact Rejection**
- High-frequency detection: Power >20 μV² in 40-100 Hz band
- Automated epoch rejection if >30% channels contaminated
- Manual review for borderline cases
- Preservation priority: Frontal/temporal gamma (relevant for Φ)

**Step 4: Eye Movement Correction**
- ICA decomposition: FastICA or Infomax algorithm
- Component identification: EOG correlation >0.8
- Automated rejection: Components with frontal topography + 0.5-5 Hz peak
- Validation: Preserve non-ocular frontal activity

**Step 5: Movement Artifact Handling**
- Amplitude threshold: Reject epochs with |voltage| >150 μV
- Gradient threshold: Reject |dV/dt| >50 μV/sample
- Flatline detection: <0.5 μV variance suggests disconnection
- Temporal smoothing: 100ms window to prevent transient rejections

**Quality Metrics:**
- Target: >80% retained epochs after artifact rejection
- Minimum acceptable: >60% retained (otherwise recalibrate)
- SNR requirement: >10 dB in 1-40 Hz band

#### Component-Specific Calculation Procedures

**Φ(t): Integrated Information Approximation**

Real-time Φ calculation requires computational shortcuts compared to full IIT analysis:

**Geometric Approximation Method:**
1. Calculate binary activity pattern (threshold at median voltage)
2. Compute channel-wise probabilities: p(x_i = 1)
3. Calculate pairwise mutual information: I(X_i; X_j)
4. Integration metric: Φ_geom = √(mean_correlation) × H(system) / H(parts)
5. Time window: 100ms epochs (balance temporal resolution vs statistics)

**Validation against IIT:**
- Full PyPHI calculation on subset of cases (N=50)
- Correlation: r>0.85 between Φ_geom and Φ_IIT
- Systematic bias correction if Φ_geom consistently over/underestimates

**Critical parameters:**
- Binarization threshold: Adaptive (median vs fixed)
- Integration scale: Local (neighboring channels) vs global
- Temporal integration: 50-200ms window sweep
- Normalization: Scale to 0-20 bit range based on channel count

**R(t): Self-Reference Completeness**

Self-reference requires recursive temporal analysis:

**Autocorrelation Method:**
1. Calculate global field power: GFP(t) = √(Σ V_i²(t) / N)
2. Compute autocorrelation: R(τ) = Corr[GFP(t), GFP(t-τ)]
3. Extract self-reference: R_SR = max(R(τ)) over τ = 50-500ms
4. Normalize: R(t) ∈ [0,1] based on maximum possible autocorrelation

**Default Mode Network Coherence Method (Alternative):**
1. Identify DMN channels: Posterior cingulate, medial prefrontal, angular gyrus
2. Calculate inter-regional phase synchrony: PLV (phase-locking value)
3. Self-reference proxy: R_DMN = mean(PLV) across DMN pairs
4. Validation: R_DMN correlates r>0.7 with metacognitive accuracy

**Hybrid approach:** R_final = 0.6×R_SR + 0.4×R_DMN
- Leverages both temporal and spatial self-reference signatures
- More robust to artifacts in either domain

**D(t): Dimensional Complexity**

Effective dimensionality from PCA:

**Principal Component Analysis:**
1. Covariance matrix C = (1/T) Σ V(t)V(t)ᵀ
2. Eigendecomposition: C = QΛQᵀ
3. Participation ratio: PR = (Σ λ_i)² / Σ λ_i²
4. Normalized dimension: D_PCA = PR / N_channels ∈ [0,1]

**Detrended Fluctuation Analysis (Alternative):**
1. Calculate cumulative sum: Y(k) = Σ[V(i) - mean(V)]
2. Divide into segments, fit linear trends
3. Fluctuation function: F(n) = √(mean[(Y - Y_fit)²])
4. Scaling exponent α from log-log slope
5. Dimensional proxy: D_DFA = 1 / |α - 1|

**Integration:** D_final = √(D_PCA × D_DFA)
- Geometric mean captures both spatial and temporal complexity

- Eyes closed
