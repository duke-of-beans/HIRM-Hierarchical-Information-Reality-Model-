# ABSTRACT

The quantum measurement problem and consciousness emergence share deep structural similarity: both involve transitions from superposition to definiteness. We propose Self-Reference-Induced Decoherence (SRID) as the mechanism bridging quantum and classical domains in conscious systems. When self-referential information processing reaches a critical threshold (C_critical ~ 8.3 bits), complete self-modeling triggers quantum decoherence without requiring external observers. This framework resolves the measurement problem internally: consciousness IS the measurement event. We derive the threshold from four independent sources (Landauer limit, Holevo bound, quantum error correction, information-induced collapse), showing convergence on ~1 bit as the fundamental measurement quantum, amplified through recursive self-reference loops. Experimental predictions distinguish SRID from competing theories including IIT, GNW, and Orch-OR.

**Stage 2 (R -> 1):** Critical self-reference. Self-model becomes sufficiently complete to include the modeling process itself. Recursive closure creates measurement-like event.

**Stage 3:** SRID event. State-space bifurcates into observer (self-referential process) and observed (modeled content). Subject-object distinction emerges.

## 4.2 Category-Theoretic Formalization

Lawvere's fixed-point theorem: In cartesian closed categories with point-surjective morphisms, every endomorphism has a fixed point. Below threshold, representation morphisms fail surjectivity. Above threshold, fixed points emerge: states representing themselves - awareness aware of awareness.

## 4.3 Why SRID Addresses the Hard Problem

The subject-object distinction created by SRID IS phenomenal experience. The system cannot model itself as not-modeling. This creates irreducible first-person perspective: a view from inside the loop that cannot be reduced to a view from outside.

The measurement creates the measurer through recursive self-reference.
