### The Problem

The multiplicative composition results validate HIRM's form but challenge its specific architecture. The equation is right. The axes and exponents need updating.

### Required Changes

**RC-01: Fit exponents empirically.**
HIRM assumes C = Φ¹ × R¹ × D¹ (unit exponents). Sleep-EDF data shows the best fit is approximately Φ^0.8 × R^0.05 × D^0.4. Unit exponents were never justified. The general theory says exponents are domain-specific and must be estimated from data.

**RC-02: Evaluate R's independence.**
R_obs (Lempel-Ziv complexity) is saturated at 0.94-1.00 across all sleep stages. Cohen's d = 1.29 (real signal) but only 4% dynamic range. Two possibilities: (a) the LZC instrument is wrong (established — permutation entropy or recurrence quantification needed), or (b) "reality discrimination" is not truly independent of Φ. If R covaries with Φ, the consciousness manifold is 2D, not 3D, and HIRM should be C = Φ^α × D^β. Zero-collapse still holds for two dimensions.

**RC-03: Invert the construction method.**
HIRM v1 was top-down: hypothesize axes → multiply. The information geometry framework says build bottom-up: start with the consciousness manifold (space of all possible conscious states), identify independent coordinates from EEG data by maximizing Fisher information structure, compute the volume element. The "equation" falls out of the geometry with exponents determined by data. Axes are discovered, not assumed.

**RC-04: Reinterpret C_critical.**
C_critical = 8.3 ± 0.6 bits was an empirical threshold. In the volume element framework, this becomes a minimum density of distinguishable states — a geometric property of the manifold below which the state-space diversity cannot sustain consciousness. More principled than an arbitrary number.

**RC-05: Reframe the 22 predictions.**
Most predictions depend on the multiplicative form (zero-collapse, phase transitions, cliff degradation), not on the specific axes Φ, R, D. These survive and are strengthened — they're now consequences of a proven theorem, not a hypothesized equation. Predictions that depend on R specifically (predictions about reality discrimination) need revisiting if R is eliminated or replaced.
