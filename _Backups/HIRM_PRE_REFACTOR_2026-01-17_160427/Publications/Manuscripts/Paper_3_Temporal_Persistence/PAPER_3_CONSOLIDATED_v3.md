We propose that identity information is encoded in a Persistent Information Structure (PIS) that survives dimensional bifurcation events at C_critical. Clinical evidence from disorders of consciousness, developmental trajectories showing C(t) emergence at 18-24 months coinciding with mirror self-recognition, and anesthesia hysteresis patterns support this framework. Approximately 73% of information (~6.1 of 8.3 bits) survives through consciousness transitions via multi-mechanism redundancy.

# 1. THE PROBLEM OF IDENTITY PERSISTENCE

## 1.1 The Puzzle

Every night, you lose consciousness. Neural activity patterns reorganize dramatically. Yet you wake as the same person. If consciousness depends on specific neural activity patterns, and those patterns are disrupted during sleep, anesthesia, or coma, what preserves identity across the gap?

## 1.2 Clinical Evidence

**Coma Recovery:** Terry Wallis regained awareness after 19 years, recognizing family and retaining pre-injury memories. What preserved his identity during two decades of disrupted activity?

**Anesthesia:** General anesthesia abolishes consciousness completely, yet patients wake as the same person. This happens billions of times yearly without identity loss.

**Split-Brain:** Corpus callosum severing creates two independent conscious streams, yet patients report unified sense of self.

## 1.3 Why Standard Theories Struggle

- **IIT:** Phi varies continuously; identity should fluctuate. It doesn't.
- **GNW:** Broadcasting patterns change during sleep; identity should change. It doesn't.
- **Embodied:** Bodily states change continuously; the "same body, same self" requires explanation.

## 1.4 HIRM's Proposal

Identity is encoded in a **Persistent Information Structure (PIS)** - information patterns robust to state-space bifurcations occurring at consciousness transitions. The PIS is not CCL activity but the *invariant structure* preserved when CCL reorganizes.
