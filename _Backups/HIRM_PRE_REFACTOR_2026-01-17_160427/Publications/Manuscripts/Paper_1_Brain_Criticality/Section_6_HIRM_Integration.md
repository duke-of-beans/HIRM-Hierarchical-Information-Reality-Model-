## 6. HIRM Framework: Addressing the Sufficiency Gap

### 6.1 The Complete Picture: C(t) = Phi(t) x R(t) x D(t)

The Hierarchical Information-Reality Model (HIRM) proposes consciousness emerges when three factors combine multiplicatively to exceed a critical threshold:

**Phi(t) - Integrated Information:**
Following IIT, Phi quantifies irreducible cause-effect structure. Criticality is necessary because:
- Only at criticality does global integration become possible
- Subcritical states have Phi ~ 0 (fragmented processing)
- Criticality maximizes potential Phi

**R(t) - Self-Reference Completeness:**
The degree to which a system models its own information processing:
- R = 0: No self-modeling (unconscious automaton)
- R -> 1: Complete self-reference (consciousness emerges)
- Criticality enables R -> 1 by supporting global communication required for self-modeling

**D(t) - Dimensional Complexity:**
The effective degrees of freedom in state-space dynamics:
- Low D: Simple fixed-point dynamics (no content variety)
- High D (> 7+/-2): Chaotic itinerancy becomes possible
- Criticality enables the Hopf bifurcations generating oscillatory complexity

**Multiplicative Structure:**
C = Phi x R x D means:
- All three factors must be present (any factor = 0 kills consciousness)
- High Phi alone insufficient (ant colonies have integration without self-reference)
- High R alone insufficient (simple recursive systems lack integration)
- Criticality is necessary because it maximizes all three factors simultaneously
