# ABSTRACT

The quantum measurement problem and consciousness emergence share deep structural similarity: both involve transitions from superposition to definiteness. We propose Self-Reference-Induced Decoherence (SRID) as the mechanism bridging quantum and classical domains in conscious systems. When self-referential information processing reaches a critical threshold (C_critical ~ 8.3 +/- 0.6 bits), complete self-modeling triggers quantum decoherence without requiring external observers. This framework resolves the measurement problem internally: consciousness IS the measurement event.

We derive the threshold through a novel multi-scale phase transition architecture, demonstrating how the 1-bit measurement quantum at the Landauer limit cascades through network percolation (lambda_c = 0.3), Ising criticality, and information integration to yield the macroscopic consciousness threshold. This five-level hierarchy explains why blockages at any level prevent consciousness emergence.

Critical new evidence from meditation cessation states validates SRID: practitioners voluntarily suppress self-reference (R -> 0), eliminating consciousness despite preserved neural complexity, proving that SRID requires R > 0 to trigger. Near-death experiences provide an opposite extreme, showing supercritical SRID activation during the gamma surge. Experimental predictions distinguish SRID from competing theories including IIT, GNW, and Orch-OR.

**Keywords:** quantum decoherence, self-reference, measurement problem, consciousness, SRID, multi-scale phase transitions
