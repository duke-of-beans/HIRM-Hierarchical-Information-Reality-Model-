## ABSTRACT DRAFT

The quantum measurement problem and consciousness emergence share a deep structural similarity: both involve transitions from superposition/potentiality to definite/actual states. We propose Self-Reference-Induced Decoherence (SRID) as the mechanism bridging quantum and classical domains in conscious systems. When self-referential information processing reaches a critical threshold (C_critical ~ 8.3 bits), complete self-modeling triggers quantum decoherence without requiring external observers. This framework resolves the measurement problem internally: consciousness IS the measurement event. We derive the threshold from four independent sources (Landauer limit, Holevo bound, quantum error correction, information-induced collapse), showing convergence on ~1 bit as the fundamental measurement quantum, amplified through recursive self-reference loops.

**Keywords:** quantum decoherence, self-reference, measurement problem, consciousness, information theory, SRID
