**HIRM Interpretation:**

## 2. PSYCHEDELIC STATES

### Entropic Brain Theory
**Source:** Carhart-Harris et al. 2014, Frontiers in Human Neuroscience; updated 2018

**Key Findings:**
- Psychedelics INCREASE brain entropy ABOVE normal wakefulness
- LSD, psilocybin, ketamine all show elevated Lempel-Ziv complexity
- Primary states (psychedelic) = higher entropy + criticality than secondary states
- DMN decoupling associated with ego dissolution
- Hippocampal-DMN coupling decreases under psilocybin

**HIRM Interpretation:**
- D component (complexity) can exceed normal waking values
- R component (self-reference) altered through DMN modulation
- Supports continuous C(t) above threshold
- Criticality enhanced, not reduced

### Fractal Dimension Evidence
**Source:** PubMed 2020, NeuroImage

**Key Findings:**
- LSD and psilocybin significantly increase fractal dimension of:
  - Functional connectivity networks
  - BOLD time-series
- Changes localized to dorsal attention network
- "Evolution toward critical zone" confirmed

### Clinical Proposal
**Source:** Scott & Carhart-Harris 2019, Neuroscience of Consciousness

**Key Proposal:**
- Use psychedelics to treat disorders of consciousness
- Rationale: If low complexity = unconscious, increase complexity = restore consciousness
- 1958 evidence: LSD reverses barbiturate anesthesia in cats

## 3. DEVELOPMENTAL CONSCIOUSNESS

### Fetal-Neonatal Transition
**Source:** Lagercrantz & Changeux, Pediatric Research 2009

**Key Findings:**
- Fetus: mostly unconscious, endogenous sedation
- Thalamocortical connections: formed by 26 weeks gestation
- Sleep-wake distinctions: 30 weeks gestation
- Newborn: basic consciousness (sensory awareness, self-nonself discrimination)
- Lacks reflective/metacognitive capacity (secondary consciousness)

**HIRM Interpretation:**
- R component develops postnatally
- Phi component present but limited prenatally
- C(t) gradually increases with neural maturation

### EEG Developmental Milestones
**Source:** Nature Communications 2024

**Key Findings:**
- 592 infants, 2-44 months
- Alpha peak: 9-10 Hz appears then disappears (2-6 months)
- Beta emergence: 6+ months (thalamocortical maturation marker)
- GABA anesthetics don't produce frontal alpha coherence until 10-12 months
- Sleep spindles: 2-3 months

**HIRM Interpretation:**
- Neural architecture for consciousness develops progressively
- Thalamocortical loops mature by ~1 year
- Supports gradual C_critical achievement

### Infant Anesthesia
**Source:** Cambridge PhD Thesis 2019

**Key Findings:**
- Complexity in anesthetized infants (0-3 years) REDUCED vs emergence
- Same complexity-consciousness principle applies to developing brain
- Consciousness supported by brain function "since infancy"
